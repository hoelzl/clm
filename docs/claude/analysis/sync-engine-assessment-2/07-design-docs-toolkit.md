## Summary

The seven design docs form a 13-month-compressed-into-one-month archaeology of the sync engine (2026-06-02 → 2026-07-01), and read in sequence they chart a clear causal chain where each layer patches fallout from the previous one. (1) split-voiceover-hardening (06-02) concluded the diff/apply core was sound but its perimeter leaked — and deliberately EXCLUDED voiceover companions from sync, deferring "N-file atomicity — design not started". (2) sync-voiceover-anchoring-unification (06-21) found the engine's narrative identity `(slide_id, role)` baked into 6 separate sites was too coarse; its own Phase B then discovered the proposed replacement (predecessor-anchor identity) was unstable under sibling insertion and pivoted mid-implementation to occurrence-under-slide. (3) sync-baseline-storage (06-21) is the strategic confession: the watermark exists only because a deterministic differ cannot tell "edited" from "always looked like this"; the deterministic-heuristic arc (#166→#190→#269→#364/#365/#403) "has not converged — each fix narrows the failure and exposes the next edge... the signature of approximating an underspecified problem"; it pivoted sync to agent-assist. (4) sync-agent-toolkit-redesign (06-22) executed that pivot: broke the one ~20-flag leaf command doing 7 jobs into report/verify/apply/task/accept/baseline/autopilot, removed 4 embedded OpenRouter clients from the main path, and demoted the watermark to a git-HEAD-defaulted accelerator — claiming the active bug cluster (#435/#430/#364/#366/#438) "collapses by construction". (5) One day later, sync-consistency-ledger (06-23) RE-promoted the just-demoted watermark as a git-committed per-slide trust ledger, because per-slide incremental trust over repeated reconcile rounds needs recorded state ("no commit in history is trustworthy"); its own §11 addendum corrected two concrete design errors against the real code (the proposed `.clm/` path was uncommittable; the per-slide baseline resolver was infeasible against the position-based classifier, forcing a downgrade to a "trust overlay"). (6) sync-issues-458-447-446 (06-29) fixed fallout from #429/#448 (hash normalization hard-coded "#", ledger shipped without hash_version) and added diagnose because `verify`'s flagship reconcile case is invisible to verify; its #447 conflict-policy design "was not sound as written" — 8 adversarial corrections, 3 ship-blockers, then a scope retreat to autopilot-only. (7) sync-separated-voiceover-companions (07-01) finally retrofits the companion files the 06-02 doc excluded — not by teaching the engine about them (explicitly deferred as "a second cell-identity model") but via an in-memory inline/extract projection wrapped around the entire engine, requiring uniform projection of the working tree AND all five baseline sources, a representation marker with bidirectional cache demotion, ledger recording in projected coordinate space, and four write-back safety rules.

Two meta-patterns dominate. First, every doc contains an adversarial-review section correcting its own first draft against the actual code (anchoring §2a data-flow correction; ledger §11; sync-issues per-thread corrections; companions red-team blockers) — evidence that the engine's real data flow is complex enough that even careful design passes over it are wrong on first contact. Second, identity/alignment is ad-hoc and multi-layered by accretion: cells are addressed variously by `(slide_id, role)` tuples, positions (`classify_changes`/`_baseline_index` are position-based), content anchors (#190), predecessor-fingerprint occurrence anchors (`vo_anchor`), occurrence-under-slide ordinals (`occ`), `id(proposal)` object identity for conflict decisions, and reason-STRING matching for conflict subtypes — with the ledger, watermark, report items, and companion projection each keying differently and needing explicit carry/migration logic between them.

On the maintainer's two questions, the docs themselves supply the assessment: the baseline-storage doc concedes the deterministic core is "genuinely good and must not be thrown away" (the ~90% neutral fast path), but also that "none of the three [storage options] is a clean win... we are choosing which shape of trouble to own", and the residue is irreducibly semantic. The abstractions are demonstrably not right at the identity layer: the #501 doc's admission that companion support required projecting five baseline sources, the ledger, and the watermark into a synthetic coordinate space — rather than the engine modeling what a deck IS — is the strongest single piece of evidence that cell referencing/alignment grew ad-hoc and that a systematic identity model (one canonical logical-deck representation with a single addressing scheme) would collapse much of the accidental complexity.

## Key findings

- Agent-toolkit redesign (docs/claude/design/sync-agent-toolkit-redesign.md) promised: read-by-default (bare `sync DECK` = report, never writes), emit-don't-invoke (engine frames tasks + validators, never calls a model on the agent path — 4 OpenRouter clients relocated behind `autopilot` only), 3-tier partition (mechanical/assisted/ambiguity), watermark demoted to opt-in accelerator with git-HEAD as default baseline, and 'no live LLM in CI' as a structural property. Rationale: the prior surface was one leaf command with ~20 flags doing 7 jobs, writing by default and invoking 4 embedded model clients on the main path; the active bug cluster (#435/#430/#364/#366/#429) was 'almost entirely watermark bugs'; #438 (clean committed deck reporting needs_agent=true) was 'the diagnostic tell' that the analyzer was entangled with the solver's pairing assumptions.
- The redesign claimed the bug cluster collapses 'by construction' under decision C (watermark demotion) — #438 'largely dissolves', #435 'defused', #430 'fixed', #364/#366 'mostly dissolves' (sync-agent-toolkit-redesign.md §8 table, :315-324). One day later the consistency-ledger doc re-promoted the demoted watermark as committed per-slide state, explicitly: 'This is the reframe that lets the demoted watermark be safely re-promoted' (sync-consistency-ledger.md:154).
- Companion retrofit (#501, sync-separated-voiceover-companions.md): the doc admits sync was 'structurally blind' to voiceover companions and the exclusion was 'deliberate and threaded through every layer' — discovery (`pairing.py::_is_split_slide_file:289` returns False), twin derivation (`derive_split_twin:211` → None), and a hard error in `sync.py::_resolve_single_path:200-205`. Only `clm validate` caught drift, at commit time. Native engine awareness (companions as first-class cells) is explicitly DEFERRED as 'a second cell-identity model (companion for_slide/vo_anchor vs deck slide_id) to the watermark and walker' (§6 Alternative C).
- Complexity cost of the #501 in-memory projection: (a) working tree AND all five baseline sources (git-ref, explicit-paths, detect_rename HEAD^, watermark, none) must be projected identically or divergence is 'silently swallowed' because the narrative classifier diffs each side against its OWN baseline, never DE-vs-EN directly (sync_plan.py:2587-2616 cited at §5.3); (b) a representation marker (`inlined|separated`) in sync_watermark_meta with bidirectional auto-demotion to git-HEAD cold-start on mismatch; (c) the ledger must record/compare confirmations in INLINED space or suppression silently stops working; (d) write-back needs 4 safety rules: refuse-whole-pair on any unmatched companion cell (blind re-extract would DELETE narration for renamed/removed slides), two-gate no-op detection (extract is not byte-stable: re-mints ids, re-derives vo_anchor positionally, collapses blank lines — needs a golden round-trip suite plus a semantic-equality fallback), post-apply alignment gate, and an atomic ≤4-file batch with an empty-companion tombstone; (e) read-mode purity requires cell copies because `merge_voiceover_text` mutates `vo_cell.lines[0]` in place.
- Consistency ledger (#448, sync-consistency-ledger.md): patches the problem that across repeated single-language reconcile rounds there is no per-slide baseline and 'no commit in history is trustworthy' (even the split commit — pre-split translations were hand-maintained). Machinery added: a git-committed per-topic `.clm/sync-ledger.json` keyed by slide identity; a P0 topic-directory consolidation (#453) because the proposed path was gitignored/walk-excluded and name-based exclusion cannot split `.clm/`; a four-rung trust ladder (assume < structural < agent < semantic:<model>) with `confirmed_by`/`confirmed_oracle` provenance; write gates on structural verify; id-migration carry (#454 — `ApplyResult.id_migrations` had to be newly captured because `_migrate_one_deck` mutated in place and returned nothing); #429 reflow-insensitive hashing WITH hash-versioning as a hard prerequisite; drop-on-conflict merge semantics; seed/bless/establish verbs (8 PRs: #463-#468, #470, #472); sqlite demoted to rebuildable cache — i.e., TWO stores plus git to keep coherent.
- The ledger design was wrong twice against the real code and corrected in §11: the storage path was uncommittable as written (.gitignore:267, SKIP_DIRS_FOR_COURSE path_utils.py:41), and the §4.2 per-slide baseline resolver was infeasible because `classify_changes`/`_baseline_index` are POSITION-based and a hash-only ledger cannot feed `_bundle_from_watermark` (needs position/tags/anchors/header_hashes, sync_plan.py:294-300) — forcing the downgrade to a 'trust overlay' that only suppresses re-litigation (skip if byte-stable, else fall through to the single-ref bundle).
- Causal chain across docs: (1) hardening doc (06-02) armors the perimeter, defers companions ('N-file atomicity — design not started', §3 #6/§14) → (2) anchoring doc (06-21) fixes the `(slide_id,role)` narrative-identity collapse the engine baked into 6 sites (plan index sync_plan.py:1088, baseline index :1225 where a second narrative is 'silently dropped', duplicate resolver :1128, post-apply guard sync_apply.py:582, add walk :1970, watermark role_of) → (3) baseline-storage doc (06-21) concludes the watermark itself is the root problem and pivots to agent-first → (4) toolkit redesign (06-22) executes the pivot, demotes watermark → (5) ledger (06-23) re-adds committed per-slide state on top → (6) sync-issues doc (06-29) fixes hash fallout (#458: normalize_for_hash hard-coded comment_token='#' at sync_writeback.py:257 so //-decks missed #429's benefit; the ledger shipped with SCHEMA_VERSION=1 and NO per-entry hash_version, so the token fix would silently invalidate every committed //-deck entry) and adds `diagnose` because verify misses the flagship case → (7) companions doc (07-01) retrofits what (1) excluded, via a projection that must thread through the watermark, marker, ledger, and all five baseline sources built in steps 3-5.
- Identity/alignment mechanisms in simultaneous use (the ad-hoc accretion the maintainer suspects): (slide_id, role) keyed diff for id'd cells; raw positions in classify_changes/_baseline_index; #190 content anchors (construct slugs); vo_anchor predecessor-fingerprint + occurrence ordinal (id:<sid>#<n> / fp:<hash>#<n> / tm:title#0) for companion placement; occurrence-under-slide (owning_slide_id, role, occ) for id-less narratives (chosen in anchoring Phase B AFTER the predecessor-anchor identity proved unstable under sibling insertion — 'token unstable as an identity', the test_new_shared_code_cell_copied_verbatim regression); id(proposal) object-identity keys for conflict decisions (valid only against the exact plan object — autopilot re-plans in auto-heal so keys can all miss, sync-issues correction #6); and reason-STRING matching to distinguish remove-vs-edit conflicts (correction #5: 'a future re-word silently disables the guard → destructive mis-resolution').
- Adversarial self-corrections appear in every doc, indicating design-vs-code drift is systemic: anchoring §2a ('the first draft of this doc assumed narratives travel the keyed diff; the verified reality is more split'); ledger §11 (two concrete errors); sync-issues #447 ('this design was NOT sound as written' — 8 corrections, 3 ship-blockers: the batch/directory path was entirely unwired for the primary use case, the accept-path recast matched zero proposals, remove-vs-edit was reason-string-coupled); #447 then retreated to autopilot-only v1 because the model-free reframing was 'the sole source of ship-blockers #2 and #3'; diagnose correction #1 (the canonical ID-LESS-TWIN case produces no verify violation at all, so a violation-driven classifier 'would report PASS on pairs reconcile still has work on'); companions doc folds in three red-teams' shared blocker (blind re-extract deletes narration).
- Essential complexity, per the docs' own analysis: (a) bilingual correspondence without universal ids is irreducibly semantic — baseline-storage §1: 'we have been buying each additional percent of determinism with an additional fragile rule'; (b) verify can only be structural safety, never semantic correctness ('a semantically wrong cell passes every structural invariant'); (c) no historical commit is a trustworthy sync point, so recorded trust (a ledger) or re-checking is unavoidable; (d) 4-file atomicity with crash windows is inherent to the separated-companion layout. Accidental complexity: absolute-path watermark keys (#435), position-based classifier internals that blocked the ledger design, hash normalization not parameterized by comment token, ledger shipped without hash versioning, the 20-flag mega-command, the demote-then-repromote watermark churn, three parallel cell models (slide_parser.Cell vs raw_cells.RawCell vs Studio views — diagnose correction #6; Studio hashing diverged per sync-issues Thread A correction #1), and companion support as a coordinate-transform bolt-on.
- The split-voiceover-hardening doc's verdict on the core (2026-06-02) was positive — 'the sync diff/apply core is genuinely safety-conscious and mostly correct'; brittleness lived at 4 edges + the #162 join-key fault line; its harness found every break was SILENT (0 break-loud initially), later hardened to 15 preserve / 3 break-loud / 0 break-silent. Its load-bearing thesis: de_id==en_id survives ONLY if all structural changes funnel through sync; every exit from the funnel (per-file assign-ids, hand-edited ids, extract ordering) silently breaks it — leading to hiding sharp commands as plumbing (hidden=True).
- Explicit deferred hard problems still open after all seven docs: id-less LOCALIZED both-sided drift (#365 — punted in the toolkit redesign to a tier-3 task, punted in the ledger as 'inherits its fragility', punted in #447 v1 'de-wins resolves fewer than expected... reporting the residual count is mandatory or the command over-claims success'); native N-file engine awareness (v2, 'a known ceiling'); companion crash window between os.replace calls (no journal, 'accepted v1 ceiling'); suppression of deliberately-standing one-sided narration (companions §5.5 Phase 1 finding: the §5.3 keystone prediction was WRONG for narratives — narrative-add detection is a current-state pairing gap, not a baseline delta, so a standing one-sided asymmetry always proposes propagation; fix deferred as 'an engine-wide narrative-baseline refinement'); autopilot bit-rot risk (R3); the two-trainer conflicting-watermark question ('raised by an agent; not traced', hardening §14).

## Pain points

- Watermark/baseline state has been redesigned three times in one month (hidden sqlite → demoted to git-HEAD accelerator → re-promoted as committed per-slide ledger overlay), and the end state is THREE coordinated stores (sqlite cache + committed ledger + git) plus, post-#501, a representation marker and a projected coordinate space they must all agree on. Each transition generated its own bug cluster and migration machinery (hash_version, marker demotion, id-migration carry, seed/bless/establish).
- Cell identity is not one abstraction but at least seven coexisting mechanisms ((slide_id,role) keys, positions, content anchors, vo_anchor predecessor fingerprints, occurrence-under-slide ordinals, id(proposal) object identity, reason-string subtype matching), with explicit carry/translation logic required at every boundary — and two of them (predecessor anchor as identity; per-slide baseline splicing) were tried and abandoned mid-implementation after real-code contact.
- The classifier's internals are position-based and membership-widened, which repeatedly vetoes designs: it forced the ledger down from a per-slide resolver to a skip-overlay, forces the #501 baseline projection to be uniform across five sources, and makes 'ref-splicing' impossible — a strong sign the core data model resists the per-slide/per-cell operations every new feature needs.
- Companion support is a projection bolted around the engine rather than a modeled representation: the engine still reconciles exactly one 2-file DE/EN pair, and voiceover companions exist only as a synthetic inlined text the engine never knows about, with correctness resting on 'both the working tree AND the baseline are projected the same way' plus a marker plus ledger-space discipline plus byte-instability workarounds (golden suite + semantic-equality fallback). The docs themselves call the alternative 'a second cell-identity model' — i.e., the current identity model cannot express a second file kind.
- Every design doc needed an adversarial correction pass that found first-draft errors against the real code (uncommittable paths, unwired batch paths, zero-matching accept recasts, verify-invisible flagship cases, in-place mutation leaking into classification). The consistent pattern is that the engine's actual data flow diverges from what a careful reader (or agent) infers — the same opacity the maintainer reports agents experiencing.
- Hash/fingerprint fragility is a recurring bug generator: strip()-only hash (#429) → reflow-insensitive hash → hard-coded '#' comment token (#458) → per-entry hash_version retrofit for the ledger → Studio computing hashes with a different token than the write guard (false StaleWriteError making //-deck cells uneditable). Any change to canonical hashing invalidates persisted trust in 2-3 places, each with its own versioning story.
- verify/report/diagnose overlap confusingly: verify is a symptom producer whose flagship reconcile case (id-less narrative twin) produces NO violation; diagnose had to be defined as 'a read-only superset of both verify AND reconcile detection'; report's needs_agent fired false-positives on clean decks (#438). Three read verbs answer overlapping questions with different blind spots.
- Conflict resolution (#447) exposes deep coupling: decisions keyed by id(proposal) break across re-planning; the directory/batch path (the primary use case) was silently unwired; remove-vs-edit distinguished only by reason strings; narrative conflicts unframeable (empty task bodies); id-less-localized conflicts force-defer — so the headline 'de-wins' policy silently resolves fewer conflicts than users expect.
- The autopilot verb concentrates all four embedded model clients plus all model flags plus --interactive plus --conflict plus --ledger plus --since — it inherited the old mega-command's opacity (the maintainer's 'autopilot is opaque with too many options' complaint is the designed-in consequence of decision B relocating rather than redesigning the one-shot path), and carries an acknowledged bit-rot risk (R3: no CI model runs).
- Silent-failure default: the 06-02 harness found 7/15 realistic edit flows broke SILENTLY and zero broke loudly; #501 documents that companion drift was silently unreported by every sync verb for over a year of the separated layout being the sticky default. The system's historical failure mode is silence, and each hardening pass converts a few silences to refusals — refusals which themselves accumulate (mixed decks, cross-lang asymmetry, unmatched cells, id-less conflicts) into the 'agents get stuck and fall back to manual fixes' experience.
