# Critique: the case for salvage

> Evidence slice of the 2026-07-01 sync-engine architecture assessment
> (`docs/claude/sync-engine-architecture-assessment-2.md`).
> Produced by an independent review agent; verify file:line citations against
> the current tree before reuse (tree at review time: `3980f9ab`, pre-#515).

## Position

Salvage the engine: its execution/safety spine and oracle infrastructure are the hard-won, probe-verified 60% of a sync engine that a third rewrite would have to re-earn bug-by-bug, while the actual failure mass sits in three replaceable strata — fragmented cell identity, triplicated baseline state, and accreted CLI surface — that this codebase has already proven it can consolidate in place.

## Direct answers

### Is the current design salvageable?

Yes, conditionally — the condition being that the next quarter is spent on consolidation surgery (one identity model, one baseline store, one 3-way differ, pruned surface) rather than continued per-issue patching. The execution/safety layer (resolve-then-apply purity, post-hoc fail-safes, error-gated atomic flush, hold-by-default watermark, validated bounded-LLM tiers, split/unify round-trip) is genuinely sound, verified by executable probes and field saves, and would take a rewrite months of burn-in to re-earn. The failure mass concentrates in three replaceable strata — fragmented cell identity, triplicated baseline state, and accreted CLI surface — each of which has already been successfully consolidated once in this codebase (BaselineBundle #289 P1 retired the #269/#225/#226 bug family; #403 Phase B unified narrative identity; the 1.16 re-cut removed models from the engine path with a structural test). A third rewrite re-faces the identical essential matrix, discards ~35 issues' worth of pinned landmine knowledge and the oracle infrastructure that makes any change safe, and repeats v2's burn-in (7 silent-drop/double bugs in its first 9 days) against a corpus now in production.

### Are the abstractions correct?

The macro-abstraction is right; one micro-abstraction is wrong, and most observed pain is downstream of it. Right: the layered spine (pure structural classifier → typed plan with plan-time dispositions → materialize-all-model-calls-up-front → mechanical apply → post-apply parity fail-safes → error-gated atomic flush → hold-by-default baseline advance) is the correct shape for bilingual 3-way sync and is verified — no LLM failure mode reaches disk, dry-run/apply parity is pinned, the fail-safes have a field save (#281). Wrong: cell identity is optional and heterogeneous — a cell's identity regime is selected by incidental metadata (has slide_id? narrative tag? lang attr?), yielding ≥7 coexisting schemes ((slide_id,role) keys, occurrence-under-slide, content anchors, positional ordinals, vo_anchor tokens, id(proposal) object identity, baseline-position joins) each with its own resolver, doubling-guard, and fail-safe, kept aligned by 'must mirror exactly' docstrings. Every second-order mess — the 10 parallel drift channels, 3 hand-copied state machines, 4 position spaces, plan-then-patch passes, the whole-watermark hold on id-less deferrals — is compensation for identity not being total. A systematic model is well-defined and reachable in place: (1) ONE total CellKey computed once at parse (slide_id when present; (owning_slide_id, role, occ) for narratives; (anchor, occ) for id-less cells) and carried through plan → apply → watermark → ledger; (2) ONE generic 3-way differ over the unified cell stream, per-channel semantics as data not code; (3) ONE committed content-keyed baseline (the #448 ledger finished) with the sqlite watermark demoted to a rebuildable cache or deleted. Note what stays hard even then: id-less cells under both-sided edit or reorder are genuinely ambiguous in any design that doesn't stamp ids — alert/defer is the ceiling there, and the prior assessment priced physical ids out correctly (~13k-stamp churn). The systematic model removes the accidental majority, not the essential residue.

## Argument

## 1. What is genuinely sound — and verified, not asserted

**The execution/safety spine survived adversarial probing and three weeks of daily production dogfooding.** I re-read the shipped code directly: `apply_plan` gates the atomic 2-file flush on a fully error-free pass, runs four post-hoc parity fail-safes that error "so the watermark can never advance over a corrupt state," and holds or partially advances the watermark with an explicit completeness invariant (`len(deferred_keys) == result.deferred`, sync_apply.py:598-652). The prior adversarial assessment (docs/claude/sync-engine-architecture-assessment.md, verified in full) ran **11 executable probes** against the tree: 9/11 propagated or alerted safely, **zero silent drops in the body/structure channels**, every re-probed historical fix (#269, #282, #216) held, and "no LLM failure mode reaches disk" was traced path-by-path (assessment §2.3 lens 6). The fail-safe layer has a field save: #281 was a would-be silent bug the #269 net converted into a loud error. Since the fail-safes landed, the engine's failure mode has been silent *non-propagation* (annoying, recoverable, validator-catchable) — not silent *corruption* reaching disk. That distinction is the whole ballgame for a tool that writes into a production course corpus.

**The resolve-then-apply purity contract is rare, correct engineering.** All model calls are materialized before any mutation begins; dry-run and apply share bytes through stage 2a and the parity is pinned by a dedicated test file (test_sync_dry_run_parity.py, per the tests slice). For an agent-driven tool, "the preview is the writing run" is exactly the property you want, and it was retrofitted once at real cost (#216's silent deck-doubling) — a rewrite would have to re-learn it.

**The bounded-LLM tiers are the opposite of ad-hoc.** sync_recover.py's AlignmentRecoverer sees only (slide_id, construct, hash) triples — never source — and its output must pass 6 hard gates or the region defers untouched; the cold-start verifier safe-aborts on any 'no' (sync_recover.py:227-294, per the writeback slice). `split.py` carries a declared algebraic contract (unify∘split = identity, Hypothesis-pinned). These components would plausibly *regress* in a rewrite.

**The oracle infrastructure is the salvage enabler.** I verified tests/slides/test_sync_corpus_mutation.py directly: the mutation oracle asserts the #269 cardinal invariant (propagate-or-alert, never silently drop) over real decks, now with a **bundled in-repo corpus so it runs in CI** (test_sync_corpus_mutation.py:43-48 — the post-#443 Phase B fix), falling back to the 212-pair real corpus at release time. The no-op backstop pins "clean pair ⇒ zero bytes, zero LLM." These are *behavioral, black-box* oracles — precisely the harness that makes a large in-place refactor safe, and precisely what a from-scratch engine would lack. The ~24k-LOC suite additionally encodes 189 issue references: 189 known landmine positions a rewrite would forfeit and re-discover empirically.

## 2. Why a third rewrite loses

**The essential complexity transfers wholesale.** Cross-language content can never hash-compare, so a 3-way baseline is mandatory; cells legitimately lack ids, and id-less cells under both-sided edit or reorder are *genuinely ambiguous* — the prior assessment's crux finding, which I verify still stands: alert-or-defer is the ceiling for that residue **in any design that doesn't stamp ids**, and physical ids were priced out on measured grounds (~13k-stamp churn, unify hazard, assessment §3 alternative ii). Tags are invisible to body hashes; byte-faithful round-trip and 4-file atomicity are non-negotiable. A v3 faces the identical {change-type × cell-class} matrix — and the history proves this matrix is discovered *empirically*, bug by bug, not derived up front: even the design docs' authors were wrong on first contact with their own engine in every single doc (ledger §11 corrections, #447 "not sound as written" with 3 ship-blockers, anchoring §2a). A rewrite resets that discovery clock to zero.

**We already ran this experiment.** v1→v2 was a full rewrite by the same process; v2 shipped 7 silent-drop/double bugs in its first 9 days (assessment §2.2), with re-fix chains (#216→#225→#226→e646fca5). A v3 has strictly *more* scope than v2 did (4-file companions, ledger, agent verb surface, conflict policies) and would burn in against a corpus now carrying shipped courses. The maintainer's bar — "Claude Code syncs decks manually, mostly works" — is beaten by fixing the report/loop noise in weeks, not by a months-long rewrite that must first climb back to parity.

**Honest accounting of the assessment's flip-to-C conditions.** Condition (i) — a body-channel silent drop surviving the fail-safes on a clean pass — was arguably met by #443. But read what happened: #443 was a *routing* gap (asymmetric id-ness pairing), pre-existing across all three engine generations byte-identically, found *by the assessment-commissioned corpus oracle* (P4), fixed in days, and the fix holds. The remediation loop worked exactly as designed; the miss was that the oracle wasn't in CI — which is now fixed with the bundled corpus. Condition (iii) — alert-rate unusability — is the live threat: the W10 dogfood produced 56 conflicts + 17 edits with only 3 genuine changes (~96% noise). But its root cause is not the classifier: it is the *baseline scheme* ("a consistent bilingual edit since HEAD reads as a both-edited conflict" — the docs concede most early-baseline conflicts are false), plus empty excerpts (#451, since fixed). That is squarely in the replaceable state stratum, and the #448 ledger is already the designed fix. The classifier core has never been the noise source.

**Consolidation demonstrably works here — three precedents.** (1) BaselineBundle (#289 P1), verified in the code: one baseline shape both sources produce, whose docstring states "the two sources can never again diverge in *coverage*" and names the three bug families the parallel plumbing shipped (sync_plan.py:295-312) — that family has not recurred. (2) #403 Phase B unified narrative identity onto occurrence-under-slide; the code is candid about the pivot and even keeps the demoted anchor channel actively consumed to satisfy the coverage gate (sync_plan.py:2558-2601, 2686-2688 — I verified the much-mocked `_ = (de_reordered, en_reordered)` line, and its docstring explains exactly why it exists). (3) The 1.16 re-cut removed all four model clients from the engine path *enforced by an import-cleanliness test* — a structural property. The pattern is established: each consolidation retired a bug family. The salvage plan is "do three more of these, bigger," not "hope harder."

## 3. Where salvage honestly cannot reach

- **Id-less both-sided drift/reorder stays alert-only.** No identity unification makes an identity-less cell unambiguous under concurrent divergence. A rewrite doesn't fix this either; only stamping ids would, and that trade was correctly rejected.
- **The false-conflict problem is intrinsic to git-ref baselines.** Only recorded per-slide trust (the ledger) or an equivalence oracle dissolves it — that is essential cost, and it means the ledger work must *finish*, not stall as a fourth parallel store.
- **inline∘extract non-identity** persists until a normal form is *declared* rather than emergent from extract's implementation; the compensation stack (re-extract-both, watermark-over-disk) stays until then.
- **Convergence loops on id-less deferrals** (whole-watermark hold → full re-surfacing → uncached re-translation) are fixable only *after* the identity work — they cannot be patched first.
- **The white-box test debt** (~15 private-symbol imports, ~15 per-file watermark seeders in 3 schema generations) means the identity refactor pays a real test-migration tax; pretending otherwise would sink the schedule.

## 4. Effort relative to a rewrite

Surgical consolidation: **~10-16 focused weeks**, incremental, each phase independently shippable behind the existing oracles, tool usable throughout. Rewrite: realistically **6+ months to parity** (the engine encodes ~35 issues' worth of behavior across ~25 terminal classification outcomes, plus the agent surface, ledger, and companion projection), followed by a burn-in period the v2 precedent says costs 5-10 fresh silent-drop bugs against a live corpus — during which the maintainer is back to manual Claude-Code syncing anyway. The salvage plan's worst case degrades gracefully (a phase slips, the current engine keeps working); the rewrite's worst case is a second unfinished engine beside a frozen first one.

## Concrete plan

**Phase 0 — Freeze and instrument (1 week).** Declare a behavior freeze on sync_plan/sync_apply: no new channels, detectors, or flags; a change requires a failing oracle first. Extend the corpus mutation oracle with DE-side and both-sided mutations (today EN-only) and add a multi-round convergence test (edit→sync→edit→sync must reach a fixpoint; the agent-stuck loop is currently untested). Strengthen the channel-coverage meta-test from hasattr to a behavioral assertion per channel.

**Phase 1 — Identity unification (3-5 weeks; the core surgery).** Create `sync_identity.py` as the single owning module: move role_of/hash_cell/anchor_of/row_anchor/construct_of/owning_group and occurrence indexing out of sync_writeback/anchor_primitives/sync_plan (fixes the inverted layering and the _SYNC_ROLE_TAGS duplication). Define ONE total CellKey — slide_id-keyed when id'd, (owning_slide_id, role, occ) for narratives, (anchor, occ) for id-less cells — computed once at parse, carried on every cell through plan, apply, watermark, and ledger; positions become write-locate details, never identity. Collapse the 3 occurrence-counters to one and the 2 content-hash functions to one canonical + one placement-tolerant derivative, under a single versioned HASH_VERSION with a migration. Replace id(proposal) decision keys with plan-minted stable value keys (the report item id already exists); merge decisions=/conflict_decisions= into one Decision structure with an explicit default-policy field. Deletes the #447 landmine class outright.

**Phase 2 — Plan derivation cleanup (2-3 weeks).** Replace the 3 hand-copied 3-way state machines (keyed loop :2318, _classify_narratives :2646, id-less localized :959) with ONE generic 3-way differ over the unified CellKey stream; per-channel semantics (allowed kinds, refusal vocabulary) become data. Convert plan-then-patch (6 post-passes retracting proposals by id()) into staged emission with a single resolution step and an asserted invariant: every cell classified exactly once. Expected: sync_plan.py shrinks ~40-50% (from 4,829 lines); most 'must mirror exactly' docstring contracts become shared code.

**Phase 3 — State-store consolidation (2-3 weeks).** Finish the #448 trajectory: the committed content-keyed ledger becomes THE recorded baseline; the sqlite watermark becomes a rebuildable derived cache (or is deleted — the ledger addendum's blocker was the position-based classifier, which Phase 1/2 dissolve). One baseline default across ALL verbs (ledger → git-HEAD), ending the report-vs-apply divergence, the false-conflict cycle, and the path-keying bug family (#435/#477) by construction. Delete auto-heal/--rebaseline/seed once obsolete.

**Phase 4 — Surface pruning (1-2 weeks).** Reimplement autopilot as a scripted loop over report/apply/task/accept (delete the parallel legacy codepath and its 17-way exclusion matrix; keep the verb). Delete: watermark alias group, suggest-sync, the deprecated flat plan JSON block, --no-env-file. Add MCP task/accept/apply and align the MCP report baseline with the CLI.

**Phase 5 — Companion normal form (1-2 weeks).** Declare extract's canonical form as a spec (normalize at parse), pin inline∘extract round-trip at the normal form with Hypothesis — mirroring split/unify's proven contract — then delete the compensation stack (both-halves re-extract, watermark-over-disk re-projection).

**Keep untouched:** split.py, sync_recover.py, sync_translate.py's prompts, the fail-safe layer, the atomic flush, the walker, the corpus oracles. **Total: ~10-16 weeks**, each phase shippable, the tool usable throughout, protected end-to-end by the no-op and mutation oracles.

## Risks

- Identity unification (Phase 1) changes persisted formats: one wrong hash/schema migration invalidates every stored watermark/ledger baseline corpus-wide — the #429/#458 precedent shows even a comment-token change rippled through 3 stores; budget explicit versioning + migration tests before touching canonical forms.
- The white-box test suite fights the refactor: ~15 private-symbol imports, ~15 per-file watermark seeders in 3 divergent schema generations, and the hand-keyed CHANNEL_COVERAGE registry all break on restructure even when behavior is preserved; without a dedicated test-migration budget Phase 1 stalls at 80%.
- Phase 2 can become a stealth rewrite (second-system risk inside the salvage): the single generic differ must reproduce ~25 terminal outcomes exactly; enforce the freeze rule — any behavior change requires a failing oracle first — and land it channel-by-channel behind the mutation oracle, not big-bang.
- Occurrence-based identity remains unstable under mutation; the unified differ must preserve the pre-mutation-snapshot discipline (sync_apply narrative_cells_by_key) or it re-introduces the #403 Phase B silent-mistarget class.
- Sequencing hazard with in-flight work: #448 ledger phases and #501 Phase 2/3 companion write-back are still landing; consolidating stores mid-flight risks churn — gate Phase 3 on the ledger reaching read-path parity, and fold #515's compensation stack into Phase 5 rather than parallel-patching it.
- The alert-noise flip condition remains live: if after Phase 3 (ledger-as-baseline) real reconciles still show >50% false positives, the honest fallback is the prior assessment's alternative (v) — bilingual single-source authoring with split as a render — a product decision that is cheap to execute and should be named now so salvage has a defined failure exit, not an indefinite extension.
- Oracle blind spots persist during the surgery: mutations are EN-side only today, multi-round convergence and crash-between-os.replace are untested, and the real-corpus runs execute only on the maintainer's machine — Phase 0 must close these before Phases 1-2 lean on them.
- Maintainer bandwidth is the schedule's real constraint: the plan assumes continued daily dogfooding as the field oracle; if that cadence stops, latent regressions ship (the #443 oracle was CI-invisible for weeks and fired only at release time).
