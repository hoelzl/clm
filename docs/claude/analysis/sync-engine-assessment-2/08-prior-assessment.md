## Summary

The previous assessment (docs/claude/sync-engine-architecture-assessment.md, dated 2026-06-09, tree fab89615, when the #166-rewrite engine was 9 days old) delivered verdict (B): "sound core, wrong in two identifiable places — keep the engine, do not replace it." Its reasoning: the layered spine (pure structural classifier → typed plan with plan-time dispositions → LLM materialize → mechanical apply → post-apply parity fail-safes → error-gated atomic flush → hold-by-default watermark) is "the right shape"; all re-probed historical fixes (#269/#282/#216) held; 9 of 11 executable probes propagated or alerted safely. But it found 3 live silent-drop cells, all in the tag-metadata channel, all advancing the watermark over the loss, and named the recurring defect class: "the cardinal invariant (propagate-or-alert, never silently drop) is enforced by hand-enumerated per-channel detectors, with no completeness spine." Its crux analysis: the {change-type × cell-class} matrix is intrinsic (essential complexity); the {baseline-mode} axis was an artifact of duplicated git-HEAD/watermark plumbing (accidental); the {id-state} axis a correctly-priced trade. It attached explicit flip-to-(C) conditions: (i) a body/structure-channel silent drop surviving the parity fail-safes on an otherwise-clean pass, (ii) a fourth channel-class drop after P0 ships, (iii) field evidence that alert frequency makes the tool unusable.

Remediation status: everything except optional P3 shipped within 2 days of the assessment — P0 tag-parity fail-safes + TestChannelCoverage meta-test (PR #290), P1 BaselineBundle unification deleting the parallel git-HEAD derivations (PR #296), P2 structural-translate materialization with the recoverer materialization declined by recorded decision (PR #297), P4 oracles: first non-Python engine test + the corpus mutation oracle (PR #298), and #285 upgraded from alert to mirror via the baseline-twin join (PR #299). P3 (stored base bodies → 3-up conflict UI) was never built (no base-body storage exists in src/clm/slides today); its conflict-UX goal was partially substituted by the #451 fix (conflict items now carry both CURRENT cells) and the #448 ledger (fingerprints, not bodies).

The subsequent ~3 weeks (not 6 months — assessment 2026-06-09, today 2026-07-01) partially validate and partially falsify the verdict. Validating: no silent drop has recurred in the keyed symmetric body/structure paths; the fail-safe/error-gated-flush posture produced no destructive shipped failure; the P4 corpus-mutation oracle immediately found the next real bug. Falsifying/straining: flip-condition (i) was arguably met by #443 — a body-channel silent drop ("decks already consistent", watermark advanced) of an asymmetric (id-less DE / id'd EN) voiceover companion, pre-existing across three engine generations, invisible to the P0 meta-test because that spine enumerates watermark CHANNELS while #443 was a pairing/ROUTING gap (`_reconcile_idless_idd_narratives` cancelled proposals unconditionally) inside a covered channel; the fix (PR #471) was yet another hand-enumerated alert. #458 repeated the enumeration pattern inside a fix (#429's reflow hash hard-coded the `#` comment token, missing `//` decks). #501 revealed the assessment's scope was a 2-file engine while the product is a 4-file problem (separated voiceover companions were entirely outside the engine's model — no matrix axis existed for them). #435/#477 show a second accidental-complexity family the assessment under-weighted: path-derived identity (watermark keyed by worktree-resolved path; cwd-as-project-root) silently losing baselines. And the dogfooding data bears directly on flip-condition (iii): the real W10 reconcile flagged 56 conflicts + 17 assisted edits across 52 pairs, of which only 3 were genuine changes (~96% false-positive rate among flagged cells), with empty conflict excerpts (#451) forcing hand-extraction of all 70 flagged cells — the alert/noise burden, not silent drops, dominated real agent/human experience, and it drove three more baseline mechanisms (batch --baseline/--since, #364 auto-heal, and the entire #448 per-slide consistency ledger with a 4-rung trust ladder) to be layered on within three weeks of the "keep it" verdict.

## Key findings

- Verdict was (B) 'sound core, wrong in two identifiable places' — keep the engine; the layered spine (classifier→typed plan→materialize→mechanical apply→fail-safes→gated flush→watermark) judged 'the right shape for this problem' and converging (assessment §1). Explicitly NOT (A) 'keep going as-is' because a fresh silent drop was found in half a day of directed probing and inter-bug intervals (1,2,0,4,1,0 days) were not lengthening.
- The verdict's core diagnosis: 'the cardinal invariant (propagate or alert, never silently drop) is enforced by hand-enumerated per-channel detectors, with no completeness spine' — every new channel, baseline source, or precondition interaction re-opens the class until someone enumerates that combination; called 'a fixable property of THIS implementation, not of the strategy' (assessment §1, line 20).
- Crux (essential vs accidental): {change-type × cell-class} matrix = intrinsic to the single-language-authoring promise; {baseline-mode} axis = substantially an ARTIFACT of parallel watermark/git-HEAD code paths (root cause of #269/#225/#226); {id-state} axis = a deliberate correctly-priced trade (physical ids rejected on measured grounds: ~13k-stamp churn, #190 §3) whose positional-pairing residue is inherent and alert-capped (assessment lines 15-18).
- Conditions attached / flip-to-(C) triggers (assessment §4 line 120): (i) a body/structure-channel silent drop surviving the parity fail-safes on an otherwise-clean pass; (ii) P0 shipped and a FOURTH channel-class drop appearing anyway; (iii) field evidence that legitimate authoring hits #282-family alerts so often the guard posture makes the tool unusable.
- Remediations DONE (all within 2026-06-09/10, verified in CHANGELOG 1.15-era section): P0=PR #290 (issue #289: Tier-C retag generalized to git-HEAD, _classify_neutral_tag_drift, retag-under-move alert, TestChannelCoverage meta-test); P1=PR #296 (BaselineBundle — one baseline representation, deleted 4 per-aspect _*_baseline_from_git_head fns; id-migration now active on git-HEAD baseline); P2=PR #297 (structural translate materialized; --llm-recover materialization DECLINED by recorded decision); P4=PR #298 (test_sync_non_python.py .de.cs/.en.cs engine test + test_sync_corpus_mutation.py, the corpus' first positive propagation oracle); #285=PR #299 (retag-under-reorder alert→MIRROR via reorder-invariant baseline-twin join).
- Remediation NOT done: optional P3 (store base bodies in the watermark → 3-up conflict UI). No base-body storage exists in src/clm/slides today (grep for base_bod/base bodies = 0 hits). Partially substituted later by #451 (conflict reports now carry both CURRENT cells) and the #448 ledger (per-slide fingerprints of both halves, not base bodies).
- Flip-condition (i) was arguably MET 14 days after the verdict: #443 — one-sided edit/removal of an asymmetric (id-less-DE/id'd-EN) voiceover companion silently dropped ('decks already consistent'), a BODY-channel drop on a clean pass that no parity fail-safe caught; pre-existing across three engine generations (v1.15.0, 1.16.0, post-#448 master — byte-identical failing plans). The maintainers did not flip to (C); PR #471 fixed it with another hand-enumerated alert (_alert_asymmetric_companion_drift), continuing the exact pattern the assessment criticized.
- The P0 'completeness spine' proved one-dimensional: TestChannelCoverage (tests/slides/test_sync_tag_drift.py:504) only asserts every watermark-RECORDED channel names an existing detector/fail-safe function in a hand-maintained CHANNEL_COVERAGE registry. It structurally could not catch #443 (a pairing/routing gap between the keyed diff and the anchor-diversion path, within an already-covered channel) nor #501 (whole companion FILES outside the watermark's model). The 'spine' closes recorded-but-unconsumed channels, not the general enumeration class.
- #501 (voiceover-companion blindness) exposes a SCOPE limit of the verdict: the assessment reviewed a 2-file (.de.py/.en.py) engine; its coverage matrix (assessment §2.4) has no axis for separated voiceover_*.de/.en.py companion files, and its only voiceover mention treats for_slide joins as an unaffected seam (lens 7). The 4-file product problem was never assessed; 'many commands ignored voiceover sidecars entirely' is therefore neither validated nor refuted by verdict B — it is outside its evidence base.
- #443's trigger cell class (a narrative id'd on one half, id-less on the other) also has NO COLUMN in the assessment's cell-class matrix — the matrix assumes id-state is symmetric across halves. Two of the biggest post-assessment bugs (#443, #501) were outside the assessment's own abstraction, which is direct evidence for the maintainer's 'are the abstractions right?' question: the per-cell channel×class×baseline matrix under-models cross-half asymmetry and multi-file scope.
- #458 validates the enumeration diagnosis at a smaller scale: the #429 reflow-insensitive hash fix hard-coded the '#' comment token, so //-comment decks (C++/C#/Java/TS) still misread a pure re-wrap as an edit — an enumeration miss inside a fix, requiring WATERMARK_HASH_VERSION bump to 3 and a per-entry ledger hash_version (hash canonicalization is itself versioned mutable state).
- #435/#477 form a second accidental-complexity family the assessment noted only as a forensics annoyance: path-derived identity. The watermark was keyed by worktree-resolved path (silently missed from any git worktree, #435) and cache/config discovery treated the cwd subdir as project root (a watermark/bless written from a topic dir invisible from repo root, #477). Stable content-based deck_id remains an open design item (dogfooding handover §4).
- Dogfooding (2026-06-23, real AZAV ML W10 reconcile on PythonCourses) — the false-positive burden dominated: report at a pre-edit baseline = 52 pairs, 25 needs-work, 56 conflicts + 17 assisted edits; after hand-reading all 70 flagged cells only 3 GENUINE changes existed (~96% of flagged items already consistent). Conflict items shipped EMPTY excerpts (#451, called 'the linchpin pain') so the agent had to re-open and hand-extract every cell. This is field evidence adjacent to flip-condition (iii): alert noise, not silent drops, is the lived cost.
- Dogfooding blind spot #1: the 1.16 git-HEAD default baseline makes a week of COMMITTED single-language edits read as already-consistent — the whole-week batch report said '50 clean / 1 review / 1 errored', falsely reassuring; drift only appears against a pre-edit baseline, and batch --baseline did not exist (hard-errored 'single deck pair'). Fixed PR #445; users think in timeframes not refs → #446 --since; DE-wins non-interactive conflict policy missing → #447.
- Dogfooding positives (what held): verify batching, the model-free task/accept engine, the accept body-shape guard (# %% rejection), and the #438 committed-id-less fix all worked as designed; the final production reconcile committed 4 EN files with verify 52/52 clean. The loop is functional but required an editable worktree clm install (PyPI 1.16 lacked batch --baseline) and hand-resolved git refs.
- The deepest dogfooding finding redefined the baseline problem the assessment treated as solved by P1: across REPEATED reconcile rounds baseline must resolve PER SLIDE ('X synced 3 days ago / Y two weeks / Z never') and 'no point in history is trustworthy' — spawning the #448 per-slide consistency ledger (committed .clm/sync-ledger.json, append-only trust, 4-rung ladder assume<structural<agent<semantic, verbs bless/seed/establish, id-rename carry). CLM now has THREE overlapping baseline/trust mechanisms (watermark, git-HEAD/--baseline/--since, ledger) plus #364 auto-heal — accreted within 3 weeks of the 'keep it' verdict.
- Timeline correction for the parent assessment: the 'subsequent 6 months of bugs' framing is wrong — the previous assessment is dated 2026-06-09 (engine then 9 days old), today is 2026-07-01. The entire post-verdict bug arc (#443/#446/#447/#448/#451/#429/#458/#435/#477/#364/#501) spans ~3 weeks of an engine ~4.5 weeks old, under daily dogfooding.
- The bug-history tally at assessment time: 7 shipped + 1 open silent-drop/double bugs (#166-P6, #190-item-2, #198, #216, #226, #269, #282, #285) in the engine's first 9 days, with re-fix chains (#225 re-fixed #216's gate, #226 re-fixed #225, e646fca5 re-fixed #226; #282 re-fixed positional assumptions in three earlier fixes at once) — the assessment itself called the re-fix chains 'damning of the enumeration process' while defending the strategy.

## Pain points

- Enumeration-by-bug-report persists post-P0: #443's fix is another named alert function, #458 is an enumeration miss inside the #429 fix — the 'completeness spine' (channel registry meta-test) covers only one dimension (watermark-recorded channels) of a ≥4-dimensional gap space (channel × cell-class × baseline × routing-path × file-scope).
- The assessment's abstraction itself is the ad-hoc part the maintainer suspects: a hand-drawn coverage matrix with hand-enumerated columns; #443 (cross-half id-asymmetry) and #501 (companion files) both fell outside the matrix, i.e. the failure mode moved from 'uncovered cell' to 'unrepresented axis'.
- ~8 implicit cross-module agreements documented at assessment time (role_of shared across ≥3 passes; watermark_rows/tag_map position lock-step; provider_available duplicated plan/apply; _pass_is_clean shared by 3 consumers; materialize-superset contract; anchor-uniqueness Counter guard repeated ≥4 sites) — 'adding a change-type means touching ~6 sites with nothing enforcing completeness' (assessment lens 8).
- Baseline/trust mechanism proliferation: watermark (per-cell, path-keyed, auto-advancing) + git-HEAD default + --baseline/--baseline-from/--since + #364 auto-heal + #448 per-slide committed ledger with bless/seed/establish/accept --record and hash_version self-heal — three overlapping trust stores accreted in 3 weeks; each new one exists because the previous one gave wrong answers in a real workflow.
- Path-derived identity is a recurring silent-miss source: watermark keyed by worktree-resolved path (#435), cwd-as-project-root (#477), rename/renumber breaking deck identity (stable deck_id still open, design-gated).
- Real-world signal-to-noise is the dominant UX cost: 56 conflicts/3 genuine in the one production reconcile, spurious reflow edits (#429), empty conflict excerpts (#451), spurious 'not found at ref' for decks created after the baseline — agents drown in false positives long before they hit silent drops.
- Watermark advance-over-loss remains the worst-case failure aggravator: every silent drop found (assessment's P1/P5/P9 and #443's edit case) also baked the divergence into the baseline, permanently defeating re-detection — detection then falls to out-of-band validators.
- Silent drops keep being found by directed effort, not by the test suite: the assessment's 3 by half-day probing, #443 by the corpus-mutation oracle that the assessment itself commissioned (P4) — and that oracle was CI-invisible (slow+skipif-on-corpus) until 2026-06-24, surfacing only as a release-gate detour.
