## 1. Verdict: (B) — sound core, wrong in two identifiable places

**The architecture is sound and should not be replaced.** The layered spine — pure structural classifier → typed plan with plan-time dispositions → LLM materialization → mechanical apply → post-apply parity fail-safes → error-gated atomic flush → hold-by-default watermark — is the right shape for this problem, and the evidence says it is converging: every historical silent-drop bug I re-tested (#269, #282, #216) stays fixed; 9 of my 11 probes — deliberately aimed at untested and git-HEAD-baseline cells — propagated correctly or alerted safely, including cells with no test coverage at all (id-less localized remove, intra-group neutral reorder, both baselines).

**But the design is not yet at its own ceiling, and the gap is structural, not incidental.** I found **three live silent-drop cells on the current tree**, all in one place: the **tag metadata channel**. A one-sided tag-only edit on (a) an id-less localized cell under a git-HEAD baseline, (b) a neutral shared cell under *either* baseline, and (c) an id-less cell under a concurrent group reorder (open #285 — confirmed, and worse than filed: the watermark advances over the loss) all report "decks already consistent" and **bake the divergence into the baseline**, permanently defeating the engine's own re-detection. The body/structure channels cannot do this anymore — they are covered by detectors *plus* the #269 parity fail-safes. The tag channel has detectors for exactly two cell-class × baseline combinations and **no fail-safe**, even though the watermark already records every tag set it would need (including the shared partition, which is recorded and never read).

**Crux answer.** The {change-type × cell-class} core of the matrix is *intrinsic*: any engine honoring the §1 promise (single-language authoring, no hand-managed ids, translated + shared + id'd + id-less cells in one percent-format file) must classify edits/adds/removes/moves per cell class. Two axes are not intrinsic:

- The **{baseline-mode} axis is substantially an artifact** of the two-baseline duality implemented as *parallel code paths*. #269's root cause was literally "the git-HEAD path never populated the shared/id-less/header baselines the watermark path had"; #225/#226 were git-HEAD-gate bugs; my probe P1 is git-HEAD-only. The information content of the two baselines is identical (a snapshot of cell rows); only the plumbing is duplicated.
- The **{id-state} axis is a deliberate, correctly-priced trade** (file-absent identity, #190 §3–4: physical ids = ~13k-stamp churn + unify hazard). Its residue — positional pairing of identity-less cells is unsound under reorder — is *inherent to cells that have no identity by construction*, in any design that doesn't stamp them. The #282 posture (detect the unsound precondition, alert, hold the watermark) is the correct ceiling for that residue, not a band-aid.

The recurring defect class is therefore best described as: **the cardinal invariant ("propagate or alert, never silently drop") is enforced by hand-enumerated per-channel detectors, with no completeness spine** — every new channel (tags, #198), new baseline source (#269), or new precondition interaction (reorder, #282) re-opens the class until someone enumerates that combination. That is a fixable property of *this implementation*, not of the strategy. The fix that closes the **class** (not the instance) is a channel-generic parity fail-safe: §5, recommendation P0.

**Why not (C):** the B-vs-C tie-breaker resolves cleanly. Replacing *one* load-bearing choice in situ — per-channel enumeration → a generic "every channel the watermark records is either reconciled or alerted" backstop — converts all three live drops into alerts and closes the mechanism that produced #198→#269→#282→#285. No wholesale alternative does better against the matrix (§3): physical ids erase one axis but keep tags/headers/conflicts and add a 13k-stamp migration plus permanent authoring churn; 3-way merge with stored bodies fixes nothing in the drop class (drops are *detection* failures, not merge failures); single-source projection (the existing bilingual/unify format) erases the whole problem but breaks the promise the engine exists to keep. **Why not (A):** a fresh silent-drop cell found on the current tree by a half-day of directed probing — with the watermark advancing over the loss — is exactly the kickoff's stated "damning evidence" shape, and the inter-bug interval (1, 2, 0, 4, 1, 0 days) is not lengthening. "Normal hardening, keep going as-is" is not a defensible reading when the hardening process itself (bug-driven enumeration) is the thing producing the residue.

A maintainer reading only this page: **keep the engine; ship the channel-generic parity fail-safe (closes all three live drops as a class, ~1–2 days); unify the git-HEAD baseline into a one-time watermark import (deletes the duplicated plumbing that produced #269/#225/#226, ~2–3 days); finish the two inline-model materialization holes when convenient.**

## 4. The case against (B) — steelman for (C), and why it loses

The strongest (C) case: *Three redesigns in nine days (engine rewrite #166, identity re-architecture #190, resolve-then-apply #222) and the same class still recurs — including two fresh cells found by a reviewer in an afternoon. The inter-bug interval is flat. The #282 fix is a guard that refuses work rather than doing it, with a declared residual (#285) that turns out to be worse than filed (watermark advances over the loss). The positional-pairing mechanism was **documented as an accepted residual in the #190 design** ("deliberate reorders of byte-identical boilerplate cells are invisible to content-anchors — accepted") and then bit anyway as #282 — the design team cannot keep its own residuals contained. An engine whose correctness depends on eight implicit cross-module agreements and hand-enumerated detector coverage will leak a new cell every time the corpus grows a new shape; the honest move is to stop paying and re-found on a representation where the invariant is checkable in one place.*

Why it loses, point by point. (1) The "three redesigns" were *additive layers shipping on schedule* — #190 explicitly preserved #166's contracts; #222 moved decisions earlier without changing semantics; none was a teardown, and all their fixes still hold under re-probe. That is a hardening trajectory, not thrash. (2) The flat interval is confounded by engine age: the entire history fits inside the burn-in window of a 9-day-old engine under daily dogfooding, and the severity trend — body drops → alerted structure drops → tag-only drops with an out-of-band validator backstop — is monotonically down. (3) The fresh cells I found are not evidence the *strategy* leaks; they are all in one channel whose detector coverage was never finished, and the steelman's own closing demand — "an invariant checkable in one place" — is **satisfiable inside this architecture**: the watermark already records every channel; the fail-safe layer already runs at the right point; P0 is that one place. (4) The #190 residual that "bit anyway" did so because it was *accepted silently* instead of guarded — and the fix that followed (#282) installed exactly the guard the acceptance should have carried. The failure was process (accept-without-alert), and the architecture's own fail-safe layer is the remedy, demonstrated in the field by #281 (a would-be silent bug that the #269 net converted into a loud error). (5) Every (C) replacement either re-faces the same matrix with undiscovered bugs (ii, iii), can't represent the problem (iv), or abandons the promise (v). The current design's bugs are *known, localized, alert-capped in severity, and closable as a class*; an alternative's bugs are none of those things.

What would flip me to (C): a silent drop in the **body/structure** channels surviving the parity fail-safes on an otherwise-clean pass (the fail-safe layer failing at its own job); or P0 shipped and a *fourth* channel-class drop appearing anyway (the completeness spine not holding); or field evidence that legitimate authoring hits the #282-family alerts so often that the guard posture makes the tool unusable (the ceiling being too low for the workflow, not just unmet).

## 5. Recommendation (prioritized; each closes a class, not an instance)

**P0 — Channel-generic parity fail-safe (~1–2 days; closes all three live drops).** Extend the `_flag_*` family (sync_apply.py:402–405) with a **tag-parity check** on otherwise-clean passes: keyed cells — `(slide_id, role)` twins must carry equal tag sets (always checkable, content-free); neutral shared cells — positionally-paired headers must carry equal tag sets (the unify invariant already implies it); id-less localized cells — group-scoped positional comparison where the streams align, alert where they don't (the `_grouped_neutral_map` trick). This converts P1, P9 (both baselines), and #285 into alerts with the watermark held. Then institutionalize it: a **meta-test that enumerates the watermark's recorded channels** (body hash, tags, header, order — and any future column) and asserts each has either a classifier detector or a post-apply fail-safe — so the next channel anyone adds *cannot* ship uncovered. File the P1/P9 findings as the issue that carries this. Cost of not doing: the tag channel keeps leaking, each leak baselines itself permanently, and every future metadata attribute re-opens the class.

**P1 — Collapse the baseline duality (~2–3 days; deletes the #269/#225/#226 failure space).** Replace the parallel git-HEAD plumbing (`_baseline_from_git_head`, `_shared_baseline_from_git_head`, `_idless_localized_baseline_from_git_head`, `_header_baseline_from_git_head`, and the `source` branching in `build_sync_plan` :2128–2177) with a **one-time import**: on first sync of a committed pair, run `_record_watermark`'s row derivation against the HEAD text and write it into the watermark, then execute the single watermark path. One baseline representation, two sources feeding it; the classifier loses an entire axis; git-HEAD-only gaps (P1's root cause — Tier C gated on `source == "watermark"`) become structurally impossible. Keep `_pair_is_unbootstrapped` as the gate deciding *whether* HEAD is importable vs the cold bootstrap.
